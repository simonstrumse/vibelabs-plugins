# Artifact: Cascade pack

The central internal-first deliverable. Executive email → manager toolkit → town-hall script → team-level talking points → written follow-up. IABC Handbook cascade convention.

## Structure

```markdown
# Cascade pack — <announcement title>

**Run:** <run-id> · **Announcement date:** <ISO> · **Cascade window:** <e.g. T-0 to T+72h>

## 1. Executive email — T-0 (all-employee)

Single email from CEO or business-unit leader. Cuts through. Grounded in the message-house roof + one pillar per paragraph.

**Subject:** <direct, not marketing — e.g. "An important change to how we work">

**Body:** (300-450 words)

Opening (1 paragraph): The news. Clear, single message. No burying.

Context (1 paragraph): Why. Business reason, not corporate-speak.

Impact (1 paragraph): What changes, for whom, when. Name the specific impact the largest affected segment will feel.

Path forward (1 paragraph): What happens next, who owns what, when employees hear more.

Close (1-2 sentences): Direct acknowledgment of difficulty if applicable. No hollow gratitude.

**Signed:** <CEO / business-unit leader>

## 2. Manager toolkit — T-0 (same morning, 1-2h before employee email)

Managers receive this **before** the all-employee email so they can prepare for 1:1s. Grounded in manager-persona + affected-employee-persona reactions.

### Talking points (1 page)
- 5-7 bullet talking points a manager can use verbatim or adapt.
- Each talking point grounded in either the message-house or a specific anticipated employee question.

### Manager Q&A — top 20
Questions managers will get from their teams in the first 24 hours. Each with:
- **Q:** <verbatim or near-verbatim from affected-employee persona reactions>
- **A:** <honest answer within the manager's authority to give>
- **If pushed:** <what to say + what to escalate>
- **Do not say:** <specific phrases to avoid, grounded in forbidden_phrases from any employee persona>

### Escalation path
Who to call for: legal questions, HR questions, policy interpretation, individual-case questions. Named owners + contact channels.

## 3. Town-hall script — T+0 to T+4h (depending on cadence)

Scripted opening (3 min) + moderated Q&A framework (45 min).

### Scripted opening
- **Speaker:** <CEO or business-unit leader>
- **Length:** 3 minutes
- **Structure:**
    1. Name the news (30 sec)
    2. Business rationale (45 sec)
    3. Impact on employees (60 sec — most important)
    4. Path forward (30 sec)
    5. Acknowledge + transition to Q&A (15 sec)

### Q&A framework
- **First 10 minutes:** pre-submitted questions (HR / comms curates top 5 from the Manager Q&A + submitted via internal form).
- **Next 30 minutes:** live-audience Q&A.
- **Last 5 minutes:** close + next steps.

### Anticipated live questions + model answers
Top 10 pulled from the Q&A doc with town-hall-specific framing. Grounded in rank-and-file employee persona reactions.

## 4. Team-level talking points — T+4h to T+24h

For use by directors and senior managers in team all-hands or smaller 1:1s.

- **Business-segment framing:** how this specifically lands in the recipient team.
- **Individual-contributor framing:** what this means for day-to-day work.
- **Career-trajectory framing:** what this means for growth / promotion / mobility within the organization.

Each grounded in relevant employee persona quotes from the reactions.

## 5. Written follow-up — T+72h (all-employee)

Single written note from CEO or business-unit leader recapping what was heard, what's clarified, and what comes next. Grounded in the Day-30 projection from the report.

## Cascade checklist

- [ ] Managers received toolkit ≥1h before employee email
- [ ] Executive email sent on schedule
- [ ] Manager 1:1s completed within 24h
- [ ] Town hall within 4h of employee email
- [ ] Team-level talking points distributed within 24h
- [ ] Written follow-up sent within 72h
- [ ] Pulse-check scheduled for Day-7

## Length target

~2000-3000 words total across all cascade elements.
```

## Grounding rules

- Executive email voice must pass the "does this sound like how the CEO actually writes" check — grounded in internal persona's expected framing.
- Manager Q&A questions pulled from affected-employee persona reactions, not invented.
- Town-hall scripted opening grounded in message-house roof + pillars; no marketing language.
- Team-level talking points reflect segment-specific employee persona reactions.
- Written follow-up references specific concerns raised in the report's employee-cluster reactions.
