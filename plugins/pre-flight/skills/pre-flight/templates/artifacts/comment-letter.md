# Artifact: Comment letter

Full regulatory comment-letter draft for the `regulatory-response` mode. Grounded in the reactions to the current draft, the regulator's reading, and peer-company competitive intelligence.

## Structure

```markdown
# Comment letter — <docket / consultation title>

**To:** <regulator name and office>
**Re:** <docket number / consultation reference>
**From:** <company name>
**Date:** <ISO date>

---

## Section 1 — Introduction and summary of position

(1-2 paragraphs)

Who we are, what we do that's relevant to this rule, and our overall position on the proposal (support / oppose / support with modifications).

Opening paragraph grounded in: the company's existing public positioning + factbase corporate-summary.

Summary-of-position paragraph grounded in: the message-house roof + one pillar per supporting argument.

## Section 2 — Alignment with regulatory objectives

(2-3 paragraphs)

Where the company's position aligns with the regulator's stated objectives. Grounded in: regulator persona's reaction (what they will read as supportive) + factbase regulatory-baseline section.

Lead with alignment before disagreement. Establishes good-faith engagement.

## Section 3 — Specific feedback on proposed provisions

(Sub-sectioned by provision number)

### Provision X.Y — <short title>

**Our position:** <support / oppose / support with modifications>

**Rationale:** (1 paragraph grounded in factbase + persona reactions)

**Evidence:** (2-4 bullets with factbase citations)
- <fact + source>
- <fact + source>

**Recommended modification (if applicable):** <concrete suggested language>

<Repeat per provision. 3-8 provisions typically.>

## Section 4 — Impact analysis

(2-3 paragraphs)

Projected impact of the proposed rule on: the company, the industry, the regulator's stated objectives. Grounded in: factbase industry-facts + peer-company persona reactions + customer persona reactions.

This section is scrutinized by regulator analysts. Every empirical claim must trace to a specific source.

## Section 5 — Alternatives

(1-2 paragraphs)

Alternative regulatory approaches that achieve the same objectives with different design. Grounded in: peer-company submissions (via peer personas), international comparison (if regulator persona references one).

Constructive, not dismissive. The regulator reads this section for signal of good-faith engagement.

## Section 6 — Conclusion

(1 paragraph)

Restate position, thank the regulator for the opportunity to comment, offer continued engagement.

---

**Signed:** <authorized signatory — name, title>
**Contact:** <regulatory-affairs contact>

## Length target

Typical comment letter: 2000-5000 words depending on complexity. Complex rules can run 10000+. Our default: 3000-4000 words.
```

## Grounding rules

- Every empirical claim traces to a factbase source.
- Every argument grounded in a message-house pillar or a consensus signal from the report.
- Provision-level feedback addresses the regulator persona's likely reading — if the regulator persona flagged a concern not addressed here, that's a gap.
- Alternatives section reflects peer-company persona signals on what competitors are likely to argue.
- Tone: formal, direct, good-faith. No corporate-speak. The regulator persona's forbidden_phrases apply.
