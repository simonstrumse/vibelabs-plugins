# Artifact: Message house

Single-page comms framework. Roof narrative → 3 supporting pillars → proof points per pillar. Universal in branded comms and crisis prep. The message house is the spine every spokesperson, every channel, every stakeholder meeting runs off.

## Structure

```markdown
# Message house — <stimulus title>

**Run:** <run-id> · **Posture:** <overall posture from reaction distribution — e.g. acknowledge-and-commit, defend-and-clarify, reset-and-reframe>

## Roof

<A single sentence that captures the organization's position on this stimulus. Drawn from the consensus signals in the report. Must not contradict any red_line warning. Must be defensible against the hostile cluster without being defensive in tone.>

## Pillar 1 — <one-phrase pillar name>

**Message:** <one-sentence pillar statement>

**Proof points:**
- <fact from factbase with source>
- <fact from factbase with source>
- <persona quote from a supportive or neutral persona>

**Where this pillar lands:** <which clusters will receive this well, drawn from the reaction distribution>

**Where this pillar is weakest:** <which clusters will reject or counter-frame this pillar, drawn from the hostile cluster's reactions>

## Pillar 2 — <one-phrase pillar name>

<...repeat...>

## Pillar 3 — <one-phrase pillar name>

<...repeat...>

## Bridges

For questions that don't fit a pillar cleanly, how does the spokesperson bridge?

- If asked about <topic outside pillars>, bridge to Pillar <n> via: "<bridge phrase>"
- ...

## What this message house does NOT do

<Honest accounting of the messages we deliberately do not lead with, and why. Grounded in red-line warnings — a pillar that would cross a hostile persona's red_line gets documented here, not in the pillars above.>

- <message we considered but did not include>: <why — which red_line or which persona's reaction ruled it out>
- ...
```

## Grounding rules

- The roof sentence is drawn from the consensus signals. If consensus is thin, the roof is tentative and says so.
- Every pillar lands one of the consensus signals from the report. Pillars without persona grounding are not pillars — they're wishes.
- Every proof point has a source. Either a factbase URL or a persona quote from the findings.
- "Where this pillar is weakest" is non-empty for every pillar. No pillar lands universally; honest surface of the counter-frame cluster makes the spokesperson prepared.
- Bridges are concrete phrases, not abstract instructions. "Bridge to sustainability" is not a bridge; "We recognize the legitimate concern about X, and that's exactly why we've committed to Y" is.

## Length

~500-800 words. Fits on a single page when laid out.
