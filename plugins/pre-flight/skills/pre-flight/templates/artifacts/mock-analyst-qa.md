# Artifact: Mock analyst Q&A

Full rehearsal transcript produced by the earnings-rehearsal mode's debate phase. 20-40 questions across easy / medium / hard / hostile difficulty tiers, each with a model answer and bridge phrases. NIRI earnings-prep standard convention.

## Structure

```markdown
# Mock analyst Q&A — <period>

**Run:** <run-id> · **Period:** <Q4 2026 / FY 2026 / H1 2026> · **Event:** <earnings call / investor day / guidance update>

## How to use this document

Rehearse aloud. Have the IR lead play analyst; CEO/CFO answer. Every "Do not say" phrase is specific — if any CEO/CFO answer drifts toward it, the IR lead flags. Bridge phrases are pre-loaded pivots to message-house pillars for moments when the question lands outside prepared territory.

## Easy — expected, prep doc covers

**Q1.** <question — standard macro / segment / operational question>
- **Who asks:** <sell-side persona>
- **Model A:** <answer drawn from pre-call script>
- **Source:** <reference to guidance section or factbase>

**Q2.** ...

## Medium — not on prep doc, should be

**Q5.** <question — second-order operational question not on the initial prep doc>
- **Who asks:** <sell-side persona — often a more detail-oriented analyst>
- **Model A:** <answer grounded in factbase + guidance>
- **Bridge if pressed:** "<pivot to pillar>"
- **Do not say:** <specific phrases from persona's forbidden_phrases list if the persona is adversarial>

**Q6.** ...

## Hard — likely to derail if not pre-baked

**Q12.** <question — multi-layered, requires commitment or detail company may prefer to avoid>
- **Who asks:** <buy-side persona — usually more demanding>
- **Model A:** <direct engagement — name the question, give what can be said, flag what can't>
- **Framing guidance:** <one sentence on tone>

- **MNPI check:** <explicit if the answer is adjacent to material non-public info>

**Q23.** ...

**Q28.** <comparative framing: "peer just reported X — how does that change your story?">

- **Model A:** <differentiation grounded in message-house pillars>

## Bridge-phrase library

Pre-loaded bridges to message-house pillars. The spokesperson reaches for these when a question lands outside prepared territory.

- **To Pillar 1 (<name>):** "<bridge phrase>"
- **To Pillar 2 (<name>):** "<bridge phrase>"
- **To Pillar 3 (<name>):** "<bridge phrase>"

## Questions we deliberately do not answer

- **<topic>:** <why — e.g. "forward-looking beyond current guidance horizon; standard practice not to comment">
- **<topic>:** <why — e.g. "pending regulatory review — would prejudice the process">

## Length target

~1500-2500 words depending on cast size and event type.
```

## Grounding rules

- Every question is pulled from or motivated by a specific investor / press / peer-company persona's reaction in the findings.
- Every model answer checked against every persona's red_lines and forbidden_phrases. A model answer that crosses a red_line is a failure.
- Hard / hostile questions drawn from the debate phase transcript.
- Bridge phrases come from the message-house artifact.
