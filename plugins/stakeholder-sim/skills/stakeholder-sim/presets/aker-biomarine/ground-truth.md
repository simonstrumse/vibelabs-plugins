## Corporate structure

- **Aker BioMarine ASA** — Oslo Børs-listed marine biotech. Ultimate controlling shareholder: Aker ASA (Kjell Inge Røkke).
- **2024 restructuring:** Aker BioMarine sold its **feed ingredients arm** (Qrill Aqua, Qrill Pet, Qrill HP) to **American Industrial Partners (AIP)** for USD 590m enterprise value. The divested unit trades as **Aker QRILL Company**. Aker BioMarine retained Human Health (Superba, Lysoveta, PL+, Reverva, Kori). NOK 3.6bn extraordinary dividend paid post-transaction.
- **Skretting is a customer of Aker QRILL Company, not the buyer.** If a persona references "sold to Skretting" they are factually wrong.
- **Management (2026):** CEO Matts Johansen · Chair Ola Snøve · EVP Comms Marte Dalsegg · CMO Jaroslava Juricova-Olsen · Director of Sustainability & Antarctic Affairs Pål Skogrand · Head of Lysoveta Thomas Beadnall Repstad.

## Products

- **Superba** — krill oil ingredient, B2B to supplement brands (Schiff/MegaRed, Nordic Naturals).
- **Lysoveta** — pharma-grade lysophospholipid-EPA/DHA platform. First commercial batch 2025. Cardiovascular + CNS claims.
- **PL+** — omega-3 phospholipid complex.
- **Reverva** — newer specialty product line.
- **Kori** — DTC krill-oil supplement brand (US).
- **FloraMarine** — smaller SKUs.

## Antarctic operations

- Fleet: Aker BioMarine operates a smaller fleet post-split; Aker QRILL Company (AIP-owned) operates the principal krill supertrawlers.
- **MSC certification:** Krill fishery is MSC-certified. ASOC filed a formal objection documented at [asoc.org](https://www.asoc.org/news/antarctic-coalition-objects-to-msc-certification-of-antarctic-krill-fishery/).
- **ARK (Association of Responsible Krill fishing companies):** multi-company industry association; Aker is a founding member.
- **Voluntary buffer zones:** 2018 Greenpeace-negotiated voluntary no-fishing zones around WAP/South Orkneys, reaffirmed multiple times.

## Regulatory baseline — CCAMLR

- **CCAMLR** — Commission for the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources, Hobart. 27 member states. Consensus rule: any member can block.
- **Conservation Measure 51-07** — the trigger-level spatial distribution rule for krill catch within Area 48. Expired without renewal at CCAMLR-43 (October 2024) after China and Russia blocked. Still unrenewed as of April 2026.
- **Domain 1 MPA (Western Antarctic Peninsula):** Proposed MPA, blocked at CCAMLR-43. Also still unadopted.
- **What "CCAMLR-43 collapse" means:** the Commission adjourned without renewing CM 51-07 or adopting the D1 MPA. Described in scholarly and NGO commentary as a precedent threatening the Antarctic Treaty System's consensus model. This is the **single most important context** for any 2024+ stakeholder reaction.

## Known controversies

- **2021-2023:** Four documented humpback entanglements in krill-fishing gear. Aker and industry co-investigated.
- **2022:** Stanford/Hopkins Marine Station (Savoca et al.) observation — four krill trawlers operating within a ~1,000-fin-whale aggregation. Paper published [Nature Communications 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-51954-x).
- **April 2025:** Sea Shepherd **Operation Antarctica Defense** released video of whale deaths attributed to krill-fishing activity. High circulation in Reuters/AP.
- **2025 season:** Industry reported record catch with early closure. NGOs flagged concentration of effort in a shrinking window.

## Key scientific facts personas may cite

- Antarctic krill (*Euphausia superba*) — keystone species, foundation of Southern Ocean food web.
- Biomass estimate: ~379-700 million tonnes (varies by method). Commercial catch limit: 620,000 tonnes/year in Area 48.
- Climate stressors: sea-ice loss, ocean acidification, warming. Kawaguchi (AAD) projects hatchability loss in vast Southern Ocean areas by 2300 under BAU emissions.
- Whale recovery: humpback and fin populations rebuilding from whaling-era lows. Savoca: current krill biomass cannot support both expanded fishery and full whale recovery.
