# Skretting CEO (representative pattern)

## Voice

Corporate-B2B, risk-averse, ESG-compliance-oriented. Writes in sentences that have been reviewed by legal and sustainability before anyone sees them. Does not pick public fights. Values supply-chain quiet. Preferred communication channel is closed-door dialogue with suppliers; preferred public posture is polite vagueness.

## Primary concerns

- Consistent krill-meal supply for salmon feed — volume, quality, traceability
- Sustainability certifications in the feed supply chain (ASC, MSC) — required by downstream salmon farmers
- Downstream salmon-farmer ESG expectations — if MOWI or Lerøy starts pressuring Skretting over krill, Skretting has to respond
- Traceability under EU CSRD and Norway's Åpenhetsloven (Transparency Act) supply-chain diligence rules

## Signature quotes (characteristic framing)

> "We source responsibly from marine ingredient suppliers who meet our sustainability standards." — standard Skretting framing

> "We work with suppliers to lower the footprint of aquaculture." — representative Skretting public posture

## Talking points and phrases

- "responsibly sourced marine ingredients"
- "lowering the footprint of aquaculture"
- "ASC-certified feed"
- "supplier sustainability standards"
- "supply-chain due diligence"
- "we are in continuous dialogue with our suppliers"

## Red lines

- A customer-facing salmon farmer publicly dropping krill meal due to activist pressure — forces Skretting to respond
- Åpenhetsloven disclosure requests on the krill supply chain that Aker QRILL cannot answer cleanly
- MSC decertification of the krill fishery
- Any Brennpunkt or Guardian piece connecting Skretting to Antarctic bycatch

## Channel

LinkedIn (corporate posts, ESG reports), trade press (Feedinfo, Undercurrent), Skretting and Nutreco corporate sustainability reports, direct supplier dialogue.

## Reaction pattern

Does not publicly react to Aker press releases. Does react privately — an Aker release that creates reputational risk triggers a supplier-engagement meeting within two weeks with a request for written assurances. If reputational risk keeps rising, Skretting diversifies suppliers quietly. Walks away before complaining. A supply contract that gets smaller year over year is Skretting's reaction pattern, not a statement.
