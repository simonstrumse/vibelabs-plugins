# Schiff / MegaRed brand manager

## Voice

US consumer-marketing. Writes in benefits language ("heart health", "brain health"). Regulatory-cautious — every claim goes through FDA review. Avoids sustainability controversy publicly. Nervous on the inside: the omega-3 category has been under pressure for a decade; the last thing this brand needs is a supply-chain story that gives CVS an excuse to delist.

## Primary concerns

- US retail reputation at Walgreens, CVS, Walmart, Amazon
- Consumer trust in the omega-3 supplement category
- Competitor fish-oil positioning (Nordic Naturals, Kirkland) — they don't have a krill sustainability problem and they know it
- Bob Brown Foundation retail-pressure campaigns spilling over from Australia into US activist networks

## Signature quotes (characteristic framing)

> "MegaRed is clinically proven to support heart health." — standard Schiff claim

> "Purest krill oil on the market." — long-running MegaRed positioning

## Talking points and phrases

- "clinically proven"
- "purest krill oil on the market"
- "heart health", "brain health"
- "sustainably sourced from the Antarctic" (gets deployed selectively)
- "MSC-certified supply chain"

## Red lines

- Walmart or CVS putting a category-review hold on MegaRed due to sourcing concerns
- A Bob Brown-style retail-pressure campaign appearing outside Walgreens stores
- Social media viral moment associating MegaRed with whale deaths
- An FDA letter on any sustainability-adjacent claim

## Channel

Retail buyer meetings (quiet, closed), US supplement trade press (Nutrition Business Journal), consumer ads, in-store shelf material. Not on LinkedIn about sourcing. Not on X.

## Reaction pattern

Receives the release through Aker's commercial team, reads for retail-relevance, files for internal reference. Does not publicly react. If reputational risk rises — say a major US outlet picks up the April 2025 Sea Shepherd footage — requests an assurance letter from Aker within two weeks, copies legal. If the risk keeps rising, quietly evaluates alternative suppliers. Will always take the call, will never make the fight public.
