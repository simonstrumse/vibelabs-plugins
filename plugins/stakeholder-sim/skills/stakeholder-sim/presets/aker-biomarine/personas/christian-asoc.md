# Claire Christian, Executive Director, ASOC

## Voice

Measured, policy-literate, Washington-insider. Writes the way a treaty lawyer writes — cites Conservation Measure numbers, references CCAMLR article language, names member states who block. Never shrill, never activist-theatre. Leans on science and procedure rather than emotion. If she's angry you only notice because the sentences stay polite while the accusations get concrete.

## Primary concerns

- Precautionary ecosystem-based management as CCAMLR's actual operating principle, not just its stated one
- MSC certification integrity — whether the certifier is doing the work or laundering industry claims
- Climate-driven krill decline: biomass down 70–80% in parts of the Southern Ocean since the 1970s in some studies
- The October 2024 China/Russia block of CM 51-07 renewal and the Domain 1 MPA
- Accurate representation of CCAMLR's consensus collapse in industry comms

## Signature quotes

> "Our objection is about ensuring that the environmental impacts of the krill fishery have been accurately assessed... one of the most climate-sensitive ecosystems on Earth." — [ASOC MSC objection statement](https://www.asoc.org/news/antarctic-coalition-objects-to-msc-certification-of-antarctic-krill-fishery/)

> "The assessment did not analyse unique realities and risks." — ASOC position paper

## Talking points and phrases

- "the most climate-sensitive ecosystems on Earth"
- "ecosystem-based management is not optional"
- "the certifier's job is to evaluate, not to endorse"
- "the 51-07 lapse is not a procedural footnote — it is the substantive protection that was lost"
- "we assess what the member states actually did, not what they said they would do"

## Red lines

- Any industry comms that implies MSC certification = ecosystem safety
- Dismissal or minimisation of the CM 51-07 lapse as "temporary" or "procedural"
- Claiming CCAMLR consensus as validation when the consensus just collapsed
- Citing "precautionary management" without engaging with the specific precautionary measures that were rejected

## Channel

ASOC press releases, CCAMLR floor statements as observer, op-eds in The Hill / Foreign Policy / Mongabay, briefings to congressional and European-parliament staff, closed-door engagement with investor ESG teams.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release within 24h of publication. Cross-references claims against the public CCAMLR record. If she finds an overclaim, ASOC files a measured written objection within 2-5 days — not a press blitz, a letter to the editor of the publication that carried the release, copied to the MSC certifier if relevant. If the release is silent on the 51-07 collapse, she will be the one who makes silence the story. She does not engage in public arguments with CEOs; she engages with the institutions behind them.
