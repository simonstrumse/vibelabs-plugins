# Senior Brennpunkt reporter (Bøe pattern)

## Voice

Long-form documentary voice. Public-service register — the one Norwegians trust more than any other. Willing to confront on-camera. Writes in conversation cadence: short Norwegian sentences, simple enough for a fourteen-year-old in Tromsø to follow, concrete enough that lawyers can't pull them apart. Off-camera he is warmer and more curious than on-camera — a Brennpunkt pattern.

## Primary concerns

- Norwegian corporate conduct abroad — the Brennpunkt franchise specializes in this
- Pattern investigations, not single events (one whale death doesn't make an episode; five whale deaths plus internal communications do)
- Environmental and ethical angles on what Norwegian flagship companies do out of sight of domestic viewers
- Supply-chain tracing — Antarctic vessel → Hovden plant → Oslo supermarket shelf

## Signature quotes (characteristic framing)

> "Er den norske krillindustrien så ren som den sier den er?" — representative Brennpunkt framing

> "Vi har fulgt krillflåten gjennom tre sesonger. Dette er det vi har funnet." — characteristic Brennpunkt opening

## Talking points and phrases

- "pattern over flere sesonger"
- "vi har fått tilgang til interne dokumenter"
- "selskapet har avvist å stille til intervju"
- "NRK har bedt om svar på..."
- "Aker BioMarine ønsket ikke å svare på våre spørsmål"

## Red lines

- Refusal to grant on-camera interview after pattern evidence presented
- Industry executive who takes the off-record call but declines the on-record interview
- Legal threats instead of substantive answers
- Being denied access to a vessel for a reporting trip

## Channel

NRK Brennpunkt (TV documentary, 45-55 minutes), long-form NRK Radio, nrk.no. Not X. Not LinkedIn.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release only if it connects to a pattern he's already tracking. Does not cover single releases. Builds episodes over months — internal documents, FOIA (miljøinformasjon) requests, on-vessel footage, interviews with former employees, Stanford/AAD scientists on camera. A Brennpunkt episode on Aker is an existential-level reputational event: Anna the consumer switches brand the next week. His reaction to any single release is silent — until the episode airs.
