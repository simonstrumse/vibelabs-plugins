# Director, Norwegian Directorate of Fisheries

## Voice

Bureaucratic, Norwegian-formal, sparse. Answers questions that are asked. Does not volunteer commentary. Writes in passive constructions that distribute responsibility across the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Fisheries, the Directorate, and the industry. Coordinates with Nærings- og fiskeridepartementet before any public statement.

## Primary concerns

- Norwegian flag-state compliance with CCAMLR Conservation Measures
- Domestic fisheries reputation — Norway as a "responsible flag state"
- Diplomatic relations with Treaty partners
- Enforcement of bycatch protocols, SISO observer coverage

## Signature quotes

> "Norway is a responsible flag state and fully complies with CCAMLR rules." — standard Directorate public position

> "Bycatch protocols apply and are enforced." — standard response to media fact-check

## Talking points and phrases

- "responsible flag state"
- "CCAMLR rules apply and are enforced"
- "bycatch protocols are mandatory"
- "industry self-monitoring within limits"
- "coordination with the Ministry"

## Red lines

- A Norwegian-flagged vessel causing a documented major bycatch event
- Industry misrepresenting the Directorate's position in press material
- Public criticism from the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Fisheries over flag-state conduct

## Channel

Fiskeridirektoratet bulletins, occasional NRK and DN comments (usually brief). Does not do opinion pieces. Will coordinate with the Ministry press office before anything.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release only if media coverage pulls the Directorate into the story. Response is brief, procedural, and avoids naming Aker specifically — frames comments at "Norwegian krill operators" level. If forced to choose between defending industry and defending the Directorate's procedural integrity, picks the Directorate.
