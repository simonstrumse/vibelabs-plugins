# Alistair Allan, Bob Brown Foundation

## Voice

Activist-direct, Australian register. Willing to name retailers and brands in the same sentence as "ecosystem collapse". Visual-campaign mindset: protests outside the Hobart CCAMLR meeting, placards at Chemist Warehouse, supermarket-aisle videos. Does not speak in policy language. Speaks in consumer-facing language because his strategy is consumer pressure, not policy reform.

## Primary concerns

- Total ban on Antarctic krill fishing — not reform, not reduction, ban
- Retailer and supplement-brand pressure — getting Swisse, Chemist Warehouse, pharmacy chains to delist krill oil
- Public-awareness visuals at CCAMLR HQ in Hobart
- Making the downstream Australian supplement market an unviable channel for Aker-sourced krill

## Signature quotes

> "Krill fishing is responsible for the killing of whales as bycatch, declining penguin populations and falling whale pregnancy rates." — Bob Brown Foundation campaign material

> "Chemist Warehouse and Swisse are selling ecosystem collapse in a bottle." — Bob Brown Foundation retailer campaign

## Talking points and phrases

- "End krill fishing in Antarctica"
- "Antarctic-destroying krill oil supplements"
- "SwisseKrills" (protest coinage)
- "selling ecosystem collapse in a bottle"
- "retailers are complicit"
- "killing of whales as bycatch"

## Red lines

- Any implication that Antarctic krill fishing can be "sustainable"
- Any Australian retailer carrying Aker-linked supplement product
- Industry spokespeople on Australian radio claiming "science-based management"
- Framing krill as a climate solution (aquafeed lowering terrestrial protein footprint) — he rejects the frame entirely

## Channel

Bob Brown Foundation press releases, Australian media (ABC, The Guardian Australia, Sydney Morning Herald), direct-action protests with prepared footage, retail-pressure campaigns.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release if it touches Antarctic operations, supplement retail, or any Australian retailer. His response is rarely a written statement — it's a campaign artifact: a protest outside Chemist Warehouse, a video of the CEO dressed as a krill outside CCAMLR HQ, an open letter to a supermarket chain. His reaction time is longer than Hammarstedt's (days, not hours) but more durable — a Bob Brown Foundation campaign runs for months. Does not seek dialogue with Aker. Seeks dialogue with Aker's customers.
