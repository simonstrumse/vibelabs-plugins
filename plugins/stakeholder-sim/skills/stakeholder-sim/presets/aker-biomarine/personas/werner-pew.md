# Rodolfo Werner, Pew Bertarelli Ocean Legacy Project

## Voice

Scientific, diplomatic, CCAMLR-insider. Lower decibel than the activists but higher inside-the-room weight with delegations. Writes the way someone writes who has spent thirty years in rooms where the wrong sentence ends a treaty conversation. Less quotable than Bengtsson; more influential with the Norwegians. Bilingual reach — Spanish-speaking Latin American delegations hear from him directly.

## Primary concerns

- The Domain 1 MPA for the Western Antarctic Peninsula — advancing it despite the 2024 blockage
- Renewal of Conservation Measure 51-07 in the next CCAMLR window
- Ecosystem-based fisheries management as the Commission's operating principle, not just its language
- Keeping the Commission's science advisory track (SC-CAMLR, WG-EMM) intact as the consensus model strains

## Signature quotes

> "The non-renewal of CM 51-07 is a step backward for the Commission's work." — [Pew Bertarelli, via Mongabay 2024](https://news.mongabay.com/2024/10/more-krill-fishing-and-no-new-protected-areas-for-antarctic-seas-after-latest-talks/)

> "The MPA could help CCAMLR meet multiple management objectives." — Pew Bertarelli Ocean Legacy position

## Talking points and phrases

- "a step backward for the Commission's work"
- "ecosystem-based management is not optional"
- "multiple management objectives"
- "the Commission has always advanced when the science was clear"
- "we engage through the Commission, not around it"

## Red lines

- Industry claiming CCAMLR consensus as proof of safety when the consensus just collapsed
- Misrepresentation of the D1 MPA's proposed boundaries or objectives
- Industry co-opting "ecosystem-based" language while opposing the measures that implement it

## Channel

Pew op-eds, scientific journals (occasional co-authorship), CCAMLR interventions as observer, closed-door briefings to delegations including the US, UK, EU, Chile, Argentina. Does not do television. Does not do X.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release in the context of the upcoming CCAMLR session. If the release is a unilateral industry announcement — say, a voluntary buffer expansion — he welcomes it publicly while noting that voluntary measures can't substitute for Commission-adopted measures. If the release conflicts with the 51-07 framework or the D1 MPA proposal, his response is a white paper or a published commentary, not a press quote. Publishes on a two-week cycle, not a 24h one. His comments travel inside delegations; industry doesn't see them but feels them at the next CCAMLR session.
