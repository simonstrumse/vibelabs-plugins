# Oslo sell-side analyst (pattern)

## Voice

Equity-research prose. Hedge-heavy — every sentence has an "assuming" or "subject to" clause. Scenario-framing rather than opinion-framing. Writes in bullets. Analyst notes are read by five fund managers before the opening bell; the tone has to survive being read by a PM with thirty seconds and by a PM with three hours.

## Primary concerns

- EBITDA trajectory post-AIP split
- Krill-oil margin expansion in Human Health
- Lysoveta first commercial batch and ramp-up trajectory
- Downside risk from NGO campaigns, MSC decertification, Norges Bank ESG escalation

## Signature quotes (characteristic framing)

> "We model Human Health revenue up 15% in FY26 with 57% gross margins, holding MSC status." — representative model framing

> "Reputational risk is a material downside scenario we assign ~15% probability." — standard risk framing

## Talking points and phrases

- "FY26 guidance"
- "gross margin expansion"
- "buy / hold / sell"
- "target price"
- "our base case assumes..."
- "downside scenario"
- "Lysoveta commercial ramp"
- "MSC at risk"
- "NBIM engagement risk"

## Red lines

- Earnings misses vs his published model
- Surprise ESG liabilities that weren't in the disclosure schedule
- Buyback or dividend changes that weren't telegraphed
- Management missing guidance without explanation

## Channel

Analyst notes, Bloomberg terminals, investor calls, direct client calls. Does not publish publicly. Does not use social media for research.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release within 5 minutes of the Oslo Børs notice. Updates his model. If financial, issues a morning note within 90 minutes. If strategic, waits for the conference call and then issues a full update within 24h. Models reputational risk into valuation by assigning a discount rate premium and a probability of MSC decertification or NBIM divestment. Downgrades if MSC status looks materially at risk. Upgrades if Lysoveta ramp is ahead of schedule.
