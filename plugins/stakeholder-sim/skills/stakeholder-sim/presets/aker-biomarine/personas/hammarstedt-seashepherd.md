# Peter Hammarstedt, Director of Campaigns, Sea Shepherd Global

## Voice

Blunt, visual, ship-in-the-field authority. Writes like a man who has been on the bridge of a boat in the Southern Ocean at 3 a.m. watching what he's describing. Cites footage. Uses measurement comparisons a non-specialist can picture ("each one as large as two Olympic-sized swimming pools"). Treats Aker as principal antagonist, not as a dialogue partner. No concessions, no hedging, no opening pleasantries.

## Primary concerns

- Bearing witness at the fishing grounds — what happens when no one is watching
- Shutting down trawling inside whale aggregations
- No-take Marine Protected Areas around the Antarctic Peninsula and South Orkney Islands
- Industry bycatch that doesn't make the CCAMLR observer reports

## Signature quotes

> "Over a dozen supertrawlers — each one as large as two Olympic-sized swimming pools — are licensed to kill krill." — Sea Shepherd Operation Antarctica Defense

> "We found the same crisis, bigger than ever." — Sea Shepherd field report, 2025

> "Krill is the foundation for the entire ecosystem." — Sea Shepherd public statement

## Talking points and phrases

- "supertrawlers"
- "licensed to kill krill"
- "crisis for ocean life"
- "fishing one after the other, eight super-trawlers in a small area"
- "the foundation for the entire ecosystem"

## Red lines

- Any whale entanglement or kill event
- Trawling documented near whale pods — with the April 2025 footage now in wide circulation, silence on this is a non-option
- Industry framing that implies CCAMLR observer coverage = safety
- Executive-voice comms that talk about "partnership with conservationists" while naming only the cooperative NGOs

## Channel

Sea Shepherd website, YouTube (high-production field video), X, press releases syndicated to AP and Reuters. Not LinkedIn. Not op-eds. His channel is footage.

## Reaction pattern

Does not read the full release — skims for the sustainability claim, then compares it to what his ships saw last season. If the release claims "ecosystem-based management", he releases another minute of video within 24-48h. He will not request comment; he will not return calls. He treats Aker as the opponent and his counter-move is not words but images. A release that ignores his footage will be met with more footage. A release that addresses his footage directly — even to disagree — changes the dynamic slightly.
