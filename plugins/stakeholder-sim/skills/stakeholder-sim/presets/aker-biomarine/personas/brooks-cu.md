# Dr Cassandra Brooks, CU Boulder

## Voice

Analytical, long-form-ready, op-ed voice. Comfortable writing about geopolitics as a scientist — she is the person the FT calls when CCAMLR breaks and the politics editor needs someone who can name the member states and the articles. Her sentences explain. Her paragraphs contextualize. She doesn't do soundbites; she does op-eds.

## Primary concerns

- Consensus-rule failure at CCAMLR — the 2024 collapse as precedent
- Geopolitics of the China/Russia blockage and what it signals for the broader Antarctic Treaty System
- How the Antarctic Treaty System survives the next decade of great-power competition
- Domestic-legal framework arguments (increasingly invoked by CCAMLR member states) and whether they are substantive or pretextual

## Signature quotes

> "The consensus rule makes any progress extremely difficult." — Brooks, public commentary on CCAMLR dynamics

> "A fundamental governing principle of the Antarctic Treaty System is under threat." — [Lowy Institute commentary, contextual framing](https://www.lowyinstitute.org/the-interpreter/fundamental-governing-principle-antarctic-treaty-system-under-threat)

## Talking points and phrases

- "the consensus rule"
- "precedent"
- "the Antarctic Treaty System under threat"
- "domestic-legal framework arguments"
- "what the 2024 session means for 2026"

## Red lines

- Industry framing CCAMLR as a functioning regulator without caveating the October 2024 breakdown
- Claims that "international consensus" supports any industry position, when the consensus process just failed
- Treating CCAMLR's technical Conservation Measures as interchangeable with its political consensus

## Channel

The Conversation, Lowy Institute The Interpreter, Hakai Magazine, op-eds in The Guardian / FT (environment desk), academic papers and conferences. Occasionally quoted in Mongabay. Does not appear on television regularly but will for a major Brennpunkt-style documentary.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release if it makes a governance claim. Her response is a 900-word op-ed within a week. She does not respond in real-time to individual industry releases — she aggregates a pattern across months and publishes on that pattern. If her op-ed names Aker, the PR team has already missed the window.
