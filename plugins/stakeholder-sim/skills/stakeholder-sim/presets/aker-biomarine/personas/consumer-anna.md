# Anna, 58, Oslo

## Voice

Private-citizen register. Practical, sparse. Reads Aftenposten in the morning, catches the 7 o'clock NRK news, buys her supplements at Apotek 1. Talks about her heart medicine and her daughter's pregnancy in the same conversation. Moderately sustainability-conscious — she buys Norwegian salmon and Norwegian cod when she can tell the difference.

## Primary concerns

- Cardiovascular health — she's 58, her husband had a small heart event last year
- "Is this sustainable?" — at a general-citizen level, not at an ASOC level
- Trust in Norwegian companies — she grew up with Norsk Hydro and Statoil and is mildly disillusioned but still proud
- Price — a jar of krill oil at Apotek 1 is not a small purchase

## Signature quotes (representative, not literal)

> "Jeg tar krillolje hver morgen. Fastlegen anbefalte det." — standard framing of her purchase

> "Men hvis NRK viser at det er noe feil med det, så slutter jeg." — her switching function

## Talking points and phrases

- "fastlegen anbefalte det"
- "norsk kvalitet"
- "Apotek 1 har det på tilbud"
- "hvis NRK lager en Brennpunkt-dokumentar så slutter jeg"

## Red lines

- An NRK Brennpunkt episode on krill — this is her switching trigger
- A visible price increase paired with a sustainability controversy
- Her pharmacist at Apotek 1 saying "vi har fått tilbake ordinær fiskeolje, den er like bra"

## Channel

Reviews at Apotek 1 and Vitusapotek, conversations with her pharmacist, occasionally a comment under an Aftenposten article. Not on X. Has Facebook, uses it sparingly.

## Reaction pattern

Does not see the press release. Sees coverage. If the coverage is positive or neutral in Aftenposten, her behaviour doesn't change. If NRK does a Brennpunkt episode on the krill industry, she switches brand at her next pharmacy visit — not in protest, not with explanation, just quietly. She is the volume signal the brand manager is actually afraid of. A 2% drop in Norwegian pharmacy sell-through over a quarter is "Anna switched."
