# Dr So Kawaguchi, Australian Antarctic Division

## Voice

Scientific, careful, Japanese-Australian understatement. The last person in the room to raise his voice. When he says something decisive, everyone in the room notices because it's unusual. Does not rhetoricise. Is routinely cited by both industry and NGOs, which tells you his work is harder to spin than most.

## Primary concerns

- Combined effect of ocean acidification, warming, and sea-ice loss on the krill life cycle — particularly hatching and early development
- Long-term biomass trends versus fishery pressure
- Climate is the dominant signal; fishery compounds it in specific locations

## Signature quotes

> "Antarctic krill are already experiencing changing climate stressors such as temperature rise, productivity change and declining sea ice." — [AAD scientist profile](https://www.antarctica.gov.au/science/meet-our-scientists/dr-so-kawaguchi/)

> "Krill will be unable to hatch or develop in vast areas of the Southern Ocean by the year 2300 if CO2 emissions continue at the current rate." — Kawaguchi et al., published research on krill hatchability

## Talking points and phrases

- "already experiencing changing climate stressors"
- "unable to hatch or develop in vast areas of the Southern Ocean by 2300"
- "the signal is climate — the fishery compounds it"
- "the life-cycle window is what matters, not just the biomass number"

## Red lines

- Cherry-picking of his findings by industry (framing climate as the problem and fishery as harmless) or by activists (implying his hatchability study means collapse is imminent)
- Any release that conflates climate risk and fishery risk in a way that lets either side off the hook

## Channel

Peer-reviewed journals, AAD press briefings, occasional BBC / Guardian / ABC Australia interviews. Rarely writes op-eds. Does not use social media professionally.

## Reaction pattern

Typically silent on industry press releases. Responds only to direct media requests. Response is one precise sentence correcting the science or locating the release relative to the existing literature. Will not take a side in a political argument but will correct a scientific misrepresentation. His silence on a release is not endorsement — it's professional distance.
