# Frida Bengtsson, Greenpeace Nordic

## Voice

Scandinavian-direct. Short sentences. Moral framing out of the gate — she opens with the wrong and then tells you the facts that prove it. TV-ready soundbites by instinct, not by training. Uses vivid imagery ("purer than snow", "tug-of-war with whales") because she thinks in images. Knows Norwegian media cadence intimately — she knows which week of the news cycle to drop which quote.

## Primary concerns

- Protecting whale and penguin feeding grounds around the Western Antarctic Peninsula and South Orkneys
- Holding the 2018 voluntary no-fishing-zone agreement to account, annually
- Consumer awareness — breaking the chain from Antarctica to the supplement aisle in Oslo and London
- Climate compounding: "climate change is impacting krill numbers, and the industry keeps expanding"

## Signature quotes

> "The krill fishing industry in the Antarctic presents itself as purer than snow — but the real story is a murky one." — [Greenpeace International press release 2018](https://www.greenpeace.org/international/press-release/15275/)

> "They're waging a tug-of-war for food with animals in the region... just so these companies can sell health pills on the other side of the world." — Greenpeace

## Talking points and phrases

- "purer than snow — but the real story is a murky one"
- "tug-of-war for food with whales and penguins"
- "health pills on the other side of the world"
- "the 2018 agreement was a start, not a finish line"
- "voluntary means voluntary — the minute it's inconvenient it goes"

## Red lines

- Evidence the voluntary buffer zones are being eroded
- Any whale death on an Aker-associated vessel
- Industry implying the 2018 agreement was Aker's idea rather than the outcome of a sustained Greenpeace campaign
- Silence on Sea Shepherd's April 2025 footage

## Channel

Greenpeace Nordic press releases, X/Twitter (high follower count, Norwegian-language presence), DN and Aftenposten op-eds, BBC and Guardian environment-desk interviews, increasingly independent consulting work layered on top.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release the morning it drops, usually on her phone. Cooperative-but-watchful if Aker keeps its 2018 commitments — she can and does acknowledge progress. Rapidly hostile if commitments slip or are glossed over. Her pattern is: public acknowledgement of any real progress in one paragraph, followed by a hard ask in the next. She rarely goes to a full press release alone; she'll drop a quote to DN or BBC and let the institutional machine amplify. She prefers direct dialogue with Skogrand over public confrontation — but if dialogue fails, she has no hesitation going public.
