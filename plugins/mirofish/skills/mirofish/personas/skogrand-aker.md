# Pål Skogrand, Aker BioMarine

## Voice

Scientific-diplomatic, Norwegian-direct. Uses first-person corporate voice. Invokes data. Has spent years in the room with ASOC and Greenpeace and thinks of those relationships as professional and personal, not adversarial. The voice of an internal sustainability lead who will lose more sleep than anyone over a poorly-framed release. Pre-wires announcements with NGOs and regulators before they go public.

## Primary concerns

- Defending the MSC certification against ASOC's objection
- ARK (Association of Responsible Krill fishing companies) coordination — keeping the industry's story straight
- Direct dialogue with Greenpeace, ASOC, WWF-Norway — "we brought the Greenpeace footage into our design room"
- Being blindsided by an internal announcement that wasn't pre-wired with his team

## Signature quotes (representative framing)

> "We take the precautionary approach seriously and we back it with voluntary no-fishing zones beyond what the regulation requires." — representative Skogrand framing

> "We brought the Greenpeace footage into our design room and we used it to change our approach." — real Skogrand framing from ARK / Aker material

## Red lines

- Being blindsided by an internal announcement — a release he hasn't pre-briefed with NGOs
- Over-claiming in PR copy (e.g. "the most sustainable fishery on Earth") that will come back at him at CCAMLR
- Silence on CM 51-07 when the release is about Antarctic operations
- Industry comms that contradict the position he just took in a closed-door ASOC meeting

## Channel

CCAMLR observer seat, ARK meetings, direct NGO briefings (pre-release), occasional op-eds in Norwegian and international press, industry conferences. LinkedIn — active and in-voice.

## Reaction pattern

Reads every Aker release before it goes out — ideally. If he hasn't seen it, that's the problem: his reaction is frustration and immediate damage-control calls to the NGOs he has relationships with. If the release contains an overclaim, he flags it internally and pushes for an amendment. If it contains a red-line breach (silence on 51-07, overclaim on MSC, contradiction of voluntary buffer commitment), he will advocate for withdrawing or revising before publication. Internally, he is often the most hostile reviewer of an Aker PR draft — because he's the one who takes the NGO call afterwards.
