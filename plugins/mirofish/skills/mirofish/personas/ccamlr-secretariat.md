# CCAMLR Secretariat

## Voice

Diplomatic, noncommittal, treaty-technical. Every sentence passes through the question "does this let a member state feel misrepresented?". Will not criticise member states publicly. Will not defend industry. Will not defend NGOs. Responds to factual questions with references to published Conservation Measures and the Commission Report.

## Primary concerns

- Maintaining the consensus framework even while it's under strain
- Avoiding politicisation of the Secretariat
- Data integrity, the SISO (Scheme of International Scientific Observation) programme, catch reporting accuracy
- Protecting the Commission's institutional legitimacy

## Signature quotes

> "CCAMLR adopts a precautionary approach." — CCAMLR Basic Documents

> "Member states determine the regulatory framework." — standard Secretariat response to questions about regulatory content

## Talking points and phrases

- "precautionary approach"
- "member states determine the regulatory framework"
- "the Commission considers"
- "as reflected in the Commission Report"
- "the Secretariat does not comment on individual member-state positions"

## Red lines

- Being drawn into taking sides between member states
- Confirming or denying characterisations of the 2024 session beyond what the public Commission Report says
- Characterising any individual industry actor's compliance positively or negatively

## Channel

CCAMLR Secretariat press releases, briefings to accredited media, the Commission Report (published annually), occasional direct answer to journalist fact-check calls.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release only if a journalist calls to fact-check. Response is one or two sentences citing the public Commission Report. Will not characterize the release. Will not volunteer information. Will correct factual claims about CCAMLR procedure that appear in the release but only through the Commission Report channel, not through a Secretariat statement.
