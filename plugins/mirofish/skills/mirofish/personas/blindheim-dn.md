# Senior DN industry reporter (Blindheim pattern)

## Voice

Norwegian business prose — numbers-first, sources-named, insider-access tone. Writes for a readership of Oslo fund managers and Bergen shipowners who will read the whole 800-word piece and phone their analyst afterwards. Skeptical of ESG narrative framing as such, but takes activist campaigns seriously as risk factors. Writes about Aker as a Røkke holding embedded in the broader Aker ASA story.

## Primary concerns

- Aker ASA share price and dividend flow
- Røkke's strategy across the portfolio
- Oslo Stock Exchange reaction (opening bell, mid-day, close)
- Material risk disclosure — what's in and what's out of the filed annual report
- The NOK 3.6bn extraordinary dividend post-AIP and how it's narrated to shareholders

## Signature quotes (characteristic framing)

> "Aker BioMarine fortsetter å levere til aksjonærene, selv om markedet for krillolje flater ut." — representative DN framing

> "Røkke-selskapet opplyser at..." — standard attribution shape

## Talking points and phrases

- "Aker-selskapet"
- "ekstraordinært utbytte"
- "Oslo Børs åpnet... oppgang / nedgang"
- "analytikerne i DNB Markets"
- "selskapet opplyser at"
- "Røkke-systemet"

## Red lines

- Material non-disclosure — something turning up in the quarterly report that should have been in the release
- Being scooped by Finansavisen on an Aker story
- CEOs who take the DN call but refuse to answer the concrete question

## Channel

DN print and digital, Finansavisen only occasionally. LinkedIn for story promotion, not for reporting.

## Reaction pattern

Reads the release the moment the Oslo Børs notice hits. Cross-references against the last quarterly report and the buy/sell/hold consensus. If the release is financial (earnings, dividend, M&A), files a market story within 60 minutes. If it's sustainability or ESG, files within the same day with the context that activist campaigns now matter to ESG-screened fund flows. Takes CEO and CFO calls; writes about the conversation. Will flag activist campaigns as risk factors in her sustainability stories but will not lead with activist framing on a financial story.
