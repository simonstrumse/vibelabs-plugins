# Tom Seaman, Undercurrent News

## Voice

B2B trade prose. Fast news cycle — stories are up within two hours of a release. Insider-industry sourcing — he has the CEOs' mobile numbers. Writes in deal-making language: "bolt-on", "margin expansion", "feed presence". Sustainability is a sub-plot in his coverage, not the headline. Will publish a press release with minor editing if the news is substantive.

## Primary concerns

- Capital flows in the krill and broader marine-ingredients space
- Competitor moves (Rimfrost, Neptune, China's CNFC)
- Aker QRILL Company's post-AIP strategy — what does AIP want, what's the hold period, what's the exit
- M&A in the marine-ingredients category

## Signature quotes (characteristic framing)

> "Aker BioMarine is looking at bolt-on acquisitions in the human-health category." — representative Undercurrent framing

> "AIP's operating thesis for Aker QRILL Company appears to be..." — representative analytical framing

## Talking points and phrases

- "bolt-on"
- "feed presence"
- "margin expansion"
- "category growth"
- "post-transaction"
- "the AIP operating team"
- "Aker QRILL Company versus Aker BioMarine"

## Red lines

- Being scooped on M&A news by IntraFish or SeafoodSource
- Embargoed releases that leak to competitors before his publication window
- CEOs who give IntraFish the exclusive

## Channel

Undercurrent News (paywalled, industry-insider), syndication to IntraFish and SeafoodSource occasionally. LinkedIn for story promotion.

## Reaction pattern

Receives the release, publishes a tightly-edited news item within 90 minutes if it's trade-relevant. Adds one or two sentences of commercial context (valuation comp, competitor comparison). Follows up with a call to the CEO if there's an M&A or strategy angle. Rarely chases the sustainability angle unless it's material to commercial strategy. Will publish a puff release with minimal editing — this is a feature, not a bug, for the PR team.
